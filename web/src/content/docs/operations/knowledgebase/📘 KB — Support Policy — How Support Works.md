# Support Policy — How Support Works

This article explains **how FIT Automate support works**—what channels we use, what "severity" means, and what response targets you can expect.

> Response targets listed here are first-pass targets and are not contractual SLAs. Final SLA terms, coverage windows, and any credits (if applicable) are defined in your service agreement.

---

## Who It Is For

Current clients who want to understand how to reach FIT when something needs attention, and what to expect in terms of response.

---

## What It Includes

### Support Channels

Baseline support is async:
- **Email / ticket-style support** (primary)
- Optional: a shared support workflow for agencies managing multiple client environments (partner track)

We keep support "audit-friendly": requests should be written, trackable, and easy to route.

### Support Coverage

**Baseline:** Monday–Friday (excluding holidays), aligned to Eastern Time (Toronto)

**Premium option (higher tiers):** Sev1 on-call coverage for critical incidents (see severity levels below)

### What Counts as "Support"

Support generally includes:
- Break/fix on delivered workflows and automations
- Troubleshooting a failure or degraded performance
- Restoring a workflow to its expected behavior
- Guidance on safe configuration changes (non-breaking)
- Clarifying "how it works" and operator questions

Support does **not** automatically include:
- New features, net-new integrations, or major scope expansion
- Large refactors due to upstream system changes (may require a project or change order)
- Third-party vendor remediation (FIT will help coordinate, but can't control their fixes)

Those items are handled as implementation work or a recurring automation support plan, depending on your engagement model.

### Severity Levels

**Sev1 — Critical**
Production is down or a critical business process cannot run, with no viable workaround.
Examples: intake pipeline fails completely, a core automation repeatedly errors, a key workflow is unusable.

**Sev2 — High**
Major degradation or repeated failures with a partial workaround available.
Examples: automation runs but misses key steps, delays cause operational disruption.

**Sev3 — Medium**
Limited impact, non-urgent bug, or workflow edge case.
Examples: formatting issue, occasional misrouting, minor logic mismatch.

**Sev4 — Low / Request**
How-to questions, small adjustments, or enhancement requests.

### Response Targets (First-Pass, Non-SLA)

| Severity | Response target (baseline) | Typical next step |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sev1 | Same business day (or faster on premium on-call) | Triage + mitigation plan |
| Sev2 | 1 business day | Triage + fix window |
| Sev3 | 2–3 business days | Scheduled fix or guidance |
| Sev4 | 3–5 business days | Scoped recommendation |

Resolution time varies based on complexity and third-party dependencies.

### What We Need from You (to Move Fast)

- What happened (plain language)
- When it started (date/time)
- What changed recently (if anything)
- Screenshots or error text (if available)
- Impact: who is blocked and what is affected
- Any workaround you've found

### How Incidents Are Handled

When a Sev1 or Sev2 issue is confirmed:
1. **Triage** — confirm scope and impact
2. **Mitigation** — stop the bleeding (pause, reroute, or degrade safely)
3. **Fix** — implement and validate
4. **Verification** — confirm normal operation with you
5. **Follow-up** — capture the learning and prevention step

### Premium Sev1 On-Call (Optional)

For agencies or teams with high-stakes operations, an on-call add-on provides a faster Sev1 response target, a defined escalation path, and a priority restoration workflow. Availability and terms depend on plan tier and operational readiness.

### Partner/Agency Support Boundaries

- **Co-branded model ("Powered by FIT"):** FIT provides build and escalation support; the partner owns front-line client communication by default.
- **White-label model (higher tier):** FIT can operate as behind-the-scenes delivery and support, with stricter entitlement rules and documentation requirements.

In both cases, entitlements (what's included, response targets, escalation rights) must be defined by tier.

## What It Is Not

- A 24/7 consumer-style support line
- Unlimited free changes (scope expansions are handled as separate engagements)
- A contractual SLA without a signed agreement

# Onboarding — Quick Start for New Clients

Welcome to **Forward IT Thinking (FIT)**. This Quick Start explains what happens next, what we need from you, and how we deliver fast, reliable results—without creating complexity.

Our goal: **make AI and automation low-friction and valuable from day one.**

---

## Who It Is For

New FIT clients who have confirmed scope and are starting their first engagement.

---

## What It Includes

### How Engagements Typically Start

Most clients follow a "start small, prove value, scale up" approach:

**Layer 0 — Discovery Call (Free)**
A structured conversation to understand your workflows and confirm there's a practical path to value.

**Layer 1 — First Engagement (Low friction)**
We implement an initial win quickly — a specific workflow automation, an AI assistant for a known use case, or an AI Readiness Audit that maps where to go next.

**Layer 2 — Deeper Work**
A fuller Automation Workflow Assessment, an AI Enablement engagement, or an expanded automation build. Most clients enter here after Layer 1 proves value.

You can enter at any layer, but most clients start at Layer 0 → Layer 1.

### What Happens First (After You Say "Yes")

1. **Welcome message** — confirms scope and next steps
2. **Access and info collection** — guided and minimal effort
3. **Target outcome confirmation** — we define "what done looks like"
4. **Build phase** — FIT implements the workflow or AI system
5. **Testing and review** — you validate real scenarios
6. **Launch and support** — we go live and monitor stability

You do not need to prepare anything complex—FIT guides the process end-to-end.

### What We Need from You (Minimal, Guided Inputs)

- The goal (what you want to happen automatically)
- 2–5 real examples of the work you do today
- Tools involved (email, spreadsheets, CRM, Teams, accounting, forms, etc.)
- Business rules (what counts as "valid"; what should never happen)
- Who's involved (who approves; who receives outputs)
- Timeline urgency (if any)

For AI knowledge systems, source documents are required (SOPs, policies, PDFs, notes, FAQs). For workflow automation, documents may be optional.

### Access and Security

FIT keeps access secure and minimal:
- Least-privilege access only (only what's needed for the project)
- Invites and role-based access wherever possible — no shared passwords
- Documentation of what access is granted and what it's used for
- You remain in control of access and can revoke it at any time

### Build Phase

During the build, FIT: maps workflow logic, builds the automation or AI system, connects required tools, adds safeguards (alerts, fallbacks, approvals where needed), tests with real examples, and documents how it works and what to monitor.

Typical delivery time: often **3–10 business days per workflow**, depending on complexity. Multi-system or high-governance work can take longer.

### Testing and Review

Before launch:
- FIT runs test scenarios
- You validate outputs
- We adjust logic based on your feedback
- We confirm edge cases and exception handling
- We verify approvals and human-in-the-loop steps

Nothing is deployed until you approve it.

### Launch and Handover

On go-live, you receive:
- A clear explanation of the automation (triggers, actions, outputs)
- What you'll see in your systems (notifications, records created, files generated)
- What to monitor (if anything)
- Basic troubleshooting guidance

---

## What It Is Not

- A complex onboarding process requiring technical expertise
- A project that needs weeks of prep before it starts
- A commitment to a long-term contract before you've seen results

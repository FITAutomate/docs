# Knowledge Base Governance — Public Overview

A knowledge base is only useful if it stays current. FIT Automate treats documentation like a product: it has owners, review cycles, and rules for publishing, updating, and retiring content.

---

## Who It Is For

Clients and prospective clients who want to understand how FIT keeps knowledge base and AI assistant content accurate over time.

---

## What It Includes

### Why Governance Matters

Without governance, knowledge bases drift: old answers stay visible, staff follow outdated steps, customers get conflicting information, and AI assistants answer based on stale content.

Governance ensures:
- The right content is published
- Old content is retired deliberately
- Changes are traceable
- Updates happen on a schedule, not "whenever someone remembers"

### Content Ownership

Every KB document FIT manages has:
- An **owner** accountable for accuracy
- A **review cycle** (how often it is checked)
- A **version** and last-updated date
- A defined audience (public-facing or internal only)

### Publish-Ready Standards

Before a document is considered publish-ready for public use, FIT aims for: clear scope, accurate language, safe boundaries (no sensitive internal details), consistent terminology, and no conflicting claims across documents.

If a policy detail is still being finalized, content is not published until confirmed.

### Public vs Internal Content Separation

Public-facing AI assistants and KB chat responses use only content that has been explicitly approved for public audiences. Internal procedures, sensitive operational detail, and configuration information are kept separate and never surfaced through customer-facing channels.

### Keeping Answers Current

Change control and deprecation prevent "two truths" from existing at the same time. When content changes, FIT updates the canonical document and marks older versions as deprecated so that AI assistant answers remain consistent.

---

## What It Is Not

- A formal ISO or SOC 2 certification
- A guarantee that every answer is always current (review cycles reduce drift but can't eliminate it entirely)
- A replacement for your own internal documentation ownership

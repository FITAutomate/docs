# How Delivery Works — Public Overview

FIT Automate delivers automation like a product team: every build follows a repeatable lifecycle designed for safety, clarity, and low maintenance overhead.

---

## Who It Is For

Prospective clients and current clients who want to understand what to expect from engagement start to go-live.

---

## What It Includes

### The Delivery Lifecycle

**Phase 1 — Confirm scope**
We align on the target outcome, triggers, success criteria, and guardrails ("never do X" rules). You provide the goals; FIT provides the plan.

**Phase 2 — Design the workflow**
FIT maps the workflow steps, exception handling, approval checkpoints, and logging expectations. You confirm the business rules.

**Phase 3 — Build and configure**
FIT implements the automation connected to your tools with safe defaults. You provide least-privilege access and test examples.

**Phase 4 — Test with real scenarios**
We validate normal cases, edge cases ("what if…?"), and failure cases. Nothing goes live until outputs match expectations and exceptions are handled safely.

**Phase 5 — Controlled go-live**
Typically staged: limited scope first, then expand once stability is confirmed. Prevents surprise automation.

**Phase 6 — Stabilize**
Post-launch adjustments, routing refinements, and operator clarity. This is where workflows become "quiet and reliable."

**Phase 7 — Improve (optional)**
For clients on ongoing support: steady improvements, fixes for upstream tool changes, added workflows, and periodic reviews.

### What You Receive at Handoff

- A clear "how it works" summary (trigger → process → output)
- What to monitor (if anything)
- What to do if something fails (operator guidance)
- How to request changes safely

---

## What It Is Not

- A one-off deployment with no support path
- Autonomous AI that runs without human visibility
- A "build it and forget it" model — real workflows need human operators who understand what's running

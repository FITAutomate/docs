# AI Readiness Audit — What It Is + What You Get

The **AI Readiness Audit** is a fast, structured assessment that helps you understand **where AI and automation will actually work in your business**—and where it won't.

It's designed to replace "AI hype" with a practical plan:

- What to do first (quick wins)
- What to avoid (risk, unclear rules, low ROI)
- What needs guardrails (approvals, exception handling, governance)
- What you need to prepare (data, documentation, ownership)

This is often the best starting point when you want momentum **without building the wrong thing**.

---

## Who It Is For

### SMB owners and operators

You want to reduce manual work, improve speed and consistency, and stop losing time to email, admin, and messy handoffs—but you don't want risky "autonomous AI."

### Agencies and delivery partners

You want a standardized way to qualify clients, scope work safely, and build repeatable offers without reinventing your process every time.

---

## What It Includes

The audit is business-first and focuses on:

- **Workflow candidates:** repetitive tasks, handoffs, approvals, reporting, intake, customer requests
- **Tools and systems:** email, docs, spreadsheets, CRM, accounting, forms, chat
- **Risk and guardrails:** what must be human-approved, what must never happen
- **Data readiness:** where information lives, quality, consistency, duplication
- **Documentation readiness:** what SOPs/FAQs exist, what's missing, what's outdated
- **Ownership and operations:** who approves, who monitors, who maintains

### Deliverables

You receive a practical pack you can act on immediately:

**1) AI + Automation Opportunity Map**
A prioritized list of the best candidates, with notes on expected outcome, risk level, effort and complexity drivers, and recommended guardrails.

**2) "Start Here" Quick Wins**
A clear recommendation for the first 1–3 wins to implement for fast ROI.

**3) Readiness Gaps**
A short list of blockers and enablers—missing SOPs, inconsistent data sources, unclear ownership, or intake routing gaps.

**4) Recommended Delivery Model**
We recommend the cleanest model to proceed: hourly (quick wins), fixed project (well-defined build), or recurring support (ongoing improvements).

**5) 30/60/90 Roadmap (optional)**
A phased plan so you can scale responsibly: 30 days (quick wins + stability), 60 days (expand across teams and tools), 90 days (governance + deeper automation).

### What you need to provide (low effort)

- Your top 3–5 pain points
- A list of tools involved
- A few real examples (sanitized is fine): emails, forms, spreadsheets, documents
- Any "never do this" rules (risk boundaries)
- Who owns approvals

You do **not** need perfect documentation to start.

---

## What It Is Not

- A full implementation project
- A technical architecture document
- A guarantee of specific ROI outcomes
- A replacement for legal or compliance advice

It's the **decision and prioritization layer** that prevents wasted effort.

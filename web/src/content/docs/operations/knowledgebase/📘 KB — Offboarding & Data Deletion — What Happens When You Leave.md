# Offboarding & Data Deletion — What Happens When You Leave

If you stop working with FIT Automate, you should be able to:

- Revoke access cleanly
- Keep the artifacts you need
- Request deletion of FIT-held copies of your materials (where applicable)
- Understand what happens to knowledge system content and automation credentials

This page is a **public-safe overview**. Exact timelines and any retention requirements are governed by your agreement and applicable laws.

---

## Who It Is For

Current clients or prospective clients who want to understand what happens when an engagement ends.

---

## What It Includes

### Offboarding Goals

- **You stay in control** of your systems and access
- **Access is removed** when work is complete
- **Client materials are handled responsibly**
- **Knowledge content is removed** from FIT-managed indexes when requested (where applicable)
- **A clear handoff** exists so you can continue operations

### What Happens During Offboarding

**1) Confirm offboarding request and scope**
We confirm which systems and automations are in scope, whether you want a full shutdown or partial pause, and whether you want data deletion from FIT-managed systems.

**2) Access revocation (least-privilege cleanup)**
We work with you to revoke FIT access to client systems: removing guest accounts and roles, disabling integration accounts created for automation, revoking tokens and keys where applicable.

> FIT does not require your passwords. Offboarding does not require you to "hand over" credentials—revocation is controlled by you.

**3) Credential and token cleanup**
Where credentials were created for automations: rotation or revocation, removal from credential stores, confirmation that automation jobs no longer run.

**4) Automation shutdown (if requested)**
We disable scheduled and inbound triggers, confirm no actions will fire post-offboarding, and provide a simple list of what was disabled and why.

**5) Knowledge system content removal (if applicable)**
If FIT hosted or managed a knowledge index for you, you can request removal of documents from FIT-managed retrieval systems (where applicable) and deprecation of any KB entries with public-facing exposure.

If your knowledge system is hosted inside your own environment, we help you identify what to disable and what to retain.

### What You Keep (Typical Artifacts)

Depending on your engagement, you may retain:
- Workflow documentation ("how it works")
- Operating notes and runbooks (public-safe versions where applicable)
- Configuration summaries (high-level, non-sensitive)
- Training materials provided to your team
- Exported templates and checklists created for your organization

If you want a full "handoff pack," request it during offboarding and we'll confirm what's available.

### Data Deletion

**What FIT can usually delete (upon request):**
- Copies of documents you provided stored in FIT-managed workspaces (where applicable)
- FIT-managed knowledge index entries derived from your documents (where applicable)
- Non-essential working files created specifically for your engagement

**What may be retained (limited and justified):**
Some records may need to be retained for legitimate business purposes: invoicing and accounting records, contractual and communication history, security or audit records required for defensibility. Retention requirements vary by jurisdiction and contract.

### How Long Offboarding Takes

Timelines depend on how many systems are integrated, whether automations are active or paused, whether knowledge systems were deployed, and whether you want a full deletion request. FIT aims to complete offboarding as quickly as practicable once access and scope are confirmed.

### Agency Partners

- **Powered by FIT:** offboarding typically occurs at the client or partner level, depending on who owns the environment
- **White-label:** offboarding may include additional steps to keep brand-facing systems stable and client communication consistent

FIT will confirm who owns what before making changes.

---

## What It Is Not

- An automatic process — offboarding requires you to initiate it
- An instant deletion for all data (some records may be retained for legal reasons)
- A guarantee of deletion from third-party systems (FIT can delete what FIT holds; third-party platform retention policies apply separately)

# Intelligent Automation — What It Is + What You Get

**Intelligent Automation** is FIT's core build and delivery service. We design and deploy reliable, reviewable workflow automations—connected to the tools your team already uses, with human-in-the-loop approvals and exception handling where it matters.

This service is for teams who are past the "should we automate?" question and are ready to build something that actually runs in production.

---

## Who It Is For

### SMB operators and teams

You have repetitive manual work you know should be automated—but previous attempts were fragile, broke quietly, or required constant maintenance. You want something stable that you can hand off to your team confidently.

### Teams coming out of an AI Readiness Audit

You've mapped your workflows, identified the quick wins, and confirmed what to build first. Intelligent Automation is the natural next step.

### Agencies looking for delivery support

You sell automation to clients but need a reliable build partner behind the scenes. FIT supports co-branded delivery so you can scale without hiring.

---

## What It Includes

### Design and scoping

- Workflow mapping: trigger → process → output → exception paths
- Guardrail definition: what requires human approval, what must never run automatically
- Tool connectivity plan: which systems need to talk to each other and how

### Build and configuration

- Automation built and connected to your tools (email, CRM, docs, spreadsheets, forms, databases, ticketing, and more)
- Human-in-the-loop checkpoints built in where needed
- Exception queues for items that fall outside normal flow
- Non-destructive defaults: nothing deletes or sends automatically without explicit design

### Testing and go-live

- Validated with real scenarios before anything touches production
- Operator guidance: what to monitor, what failure looks like, what to do next
- Staged rollout where appropriate to avoid operational disruption

### Handoff

- Documented workflow definition
- Clear change path: how to request tuning, enhancements, or scope changes after go-live
- Admin access transfer where applicable

---

## Common Automation Targets

- **Intake and routing:** web forms, chat requests, email inquiries → assigned owner and next steps
- **Inbox workflows:** triage, labeling, prioritization, task creation, draft responses (review-first, not auto-send)
- **Operations:** approvals, handoffs, reminders, exception queues, escalation paths
- **Documents:** generate, file, rename, route, classify, and extract key fields
- **CRM and sales ops:** lead capture → CRM hygiene → follow-up tasks → activity reporting
- **Reporting:** daily and weekly summaries, KPI refresh, operational rollups across tools
- **Onboarding:** client onboarding packs, internal onboarding task sequences, standardized setup flows

---

## What It Is Not

- Autonomous AI that acts without human visibility or approval
- A pre-built template deployed without configuration to your environment
- A tool replacement or platform migration
- A set-and-forget system (real workflows need human operators who understand what's running)

## How It Typically Starts

Most Intelligent Automation engagements begin with one of two paths:

**Path A — From an AI Readiness Audit**
You already have a prioritized list of workflows and guardrails. We move directly to scoping and build.

**Path B — Direct scoping call**
You have a specific workflow in mind and it's well-defined enough to scope on a call. We confirm requirements, design the automation, and begin build.

If you're unsure which path is right, an **AI Readiness Audit** is the lower-risk entry point.

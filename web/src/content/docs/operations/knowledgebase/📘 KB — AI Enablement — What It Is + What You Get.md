# AI Enablement — What It Is + What You Get

**AI Enablement** is FIT's service for helping teams adopt AI safely and consistently—so the tools you invest in actually get used, and the people responsible for outcomes stay in control.

This isn't a generic AI training course. It's structured, practical work that closes the gap between "we have AI tools" and "our team uses them well, without creating risk."

---

## Who It Is For

### Teams that have AI tools but aren't getting value from them

You've adopted Microsoft Copilot, ChatGPT, or similar tools—but usage is inconsistent, outputs vary in quality, and no one is confident about what's safe to use AI for and what isn't.

### Leaders preparing for an automation rollout

You're about to deploy automated workflows and you need the team that will own and operate them to understand the system, the guardrails, and what to do when something behaves unexpectedly.

### Organizations with governance gaps

You need clear policies around AI use—what's in scope, what requires human review, how to handle edge cases—but you don't have the internal expertise to design them.

---

## What It Includes

### AI Readiness Assessment (for enablement scope)

Before training begins, we map your current AI tool landscape, identify where usage is inconsistent or risky, and define what "enabled" looks like for your team.

### Practical Training (role-specific)

Training is built around the actual workflows and tools your team uses—not abstract AI theory. Coverage typically includes:

- How to prompt effectively for your specific use cases
- Where to apply AI and where human judgment is non-negotiable
- How to validate AI outputs before acting on them
- What to do when AI gets it wrong

### Playbooks

Documented, repeatable guidance for the most important AI use cases in your environment—so the right approach is written down and doesn't live only in one person's head.

### Governance Scaffolding

Lightweight rules and roles to keep AI use consistent over time:

- What requires a human review step
- How to handle edge cases and exceptions
- Who owns AI tool configuration and updates
- How to request changes when the rules need to evolve

### Operator Readiness (for automation rollouts)

If you're rolling out automated workflows, we prepare the humans who will own and operate them:

- What to monitor day-to-day
- What failure looks like and how to respond
- How to request changes through the proper change path
- Escalation paths for unexpected behavior

---

## What It Is Not

- A generic AI certification or academic course
- A one-time workshop with no follow-through
- A replacement for good automation design (governance supports good systems—it doesn't fix bad ones)
- IT-only work (enablement spans people, process, and tools)

---

## How It Typically Starts

Most AI Enablement engagements begin with a scoping call to understand:

- What AI tools are in use today
- Where the biggest gaps or risks are
- Who the key stakeholders and operators are
- Whether enablement is standalone or paired with an automation rollout

If you're unsure whether you need Enablement, an **AI Readiness Audit** will surface it quickly.

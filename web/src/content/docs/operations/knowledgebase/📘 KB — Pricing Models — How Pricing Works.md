# Pricing Models — How Pricing Works

FIT Automate does **not** publish fixed prices on the website or through chat. Instead, we use a clear set of pricing models and recommend the best fit based on your workflow complexity, tools, and desired outcomes.

Our pricing approach is: flexible, transparent, based on value delivered, and matched to the risk and clarity of scope.

---

## Who It Is For

Prospective clients and current clients who want to understand how FIT structures and recommends pricing before requesting a quote.

---

## What It Includes

### The 4 Pricing Models

**1) Hourly (best for quick wins and small tasks)**
Ideal when scope is small or evolving: one-time fixes, simple single-workflow automations, short consulting sessions, or testing FIT's work before scaling.

Good fit when: you want to start quickly with minimal overhead, the job is too small to scope as a formal project, or you're not yet sure what the full solution should be.

Tradeoff: harder to estimate total cost upfront if priorities aren't clearly defined.

---

**2) Fixed Project (best for well-defined workflows)**
Best when there's a clear workflow with known inputs, outputs, and scope. You get defined deliverables, a clear success definition, and a confirmed price before build begins.

Good fit when: you want predictable costs, the process is well understood, and you're implementing a specific workflow.

Tradeoff: changes mid-project may require a change order if scope shifts materially.

---

**3) Monthly Automation Support (recurring)**
Designed for organizations that want automation to grow with the business and stay reliable over time. Typically covers monitoring, reliability, fixes for upstream tool changes, ongoing improvements, and a shared priority list.

Good fit when: you have multiple workflows to build over time, want faster turnaround on fixes, or want automation capacity without hiring internal headcount.

Tradeoff: monthly commitment and requires active prioritization to stay focused.

---

**4) AI + Automation Retainer (hybrid, optional add-on)**
For organizations that want strategy and implementation capacity together — fractional automation leadership plus delivery. Used when you want to drive sustained transformation rather than individual workflows.

Good fit when: leadership wants ongoing momentum and governance, you're deploying AI systems and multi-system automations, or you want a recurring delivery rhythm.

Tradeoff: higher commitment and requires active adoption and prioritization.

---

### A Common Starting Point: AI Readiness Audit

Many clients begin with an **AI Readiness Audit** to clarify: what to automate first, risk and guardrails, complexity drivers, expected ROI, and which pricing model makes the most sense for next steps.

This prevents misaligned builds and reduces total cost by focusing effort on the highest-value workflows first.

---

### How We Recommend the Right Model

We recommend based on:
- Speed needed (how urgent is the outcome?)
- Number of processes (one workflow vs many)
- Complexity (single-tool vs multi-system)
- Need for ongoing support (do things change frequently?)
- AI involvement (classification, summarization, routing, knowledge systems)

**Simple paths:**
- **Start small:** Hourly → quick win → expand later
- **Start clear:** AI Readiness Audit → fixed project or monthly support
- **Start embedded:** Recurring support → continuous roadmap delivery

---

### What Affects Cost (Without Publishing Prices)

- Number of systems involved
- Number of steps and exception paths
- Approval and human-in-the-loop requirements
- Data quality and document readiness
- Reporting and audit visibility requirements
- Timeline urgency

---

## What It Is Not

- Fixed menu pricing (scope varies too much for that to be useful)
- A commitment before discovery (we size engagements after understanding your situation)

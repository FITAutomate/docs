# Automation Workflow Assessment — What You Get

The **Automation Workflow Assessment** is a structured, paid engagement designed to identify the best automation opportunities and turn them into a **prioritized roadmap**.

If Discovery is "Should we do this?", the Workflow Assessment answers:

- **What should we automate first (and why)?**
- **What will save the most time with the least risk?**
- **What's realistic now vs later?**
- **What guardrails and approvals are needed?**

This assessment is intentionally **business-first**, not technical-first. FIT does the heavy lifting.

---

## Who It Is For

The Workflow Assessment is a great fit when:

- You have **multiple workflows** you want to improve, but you're not sure where to start
- Your processes involve **more than one system** (email + CRM + spreadsheets + accounting, etc.)
- You want automation done **safely** (approvals, clear boundaries, no surprises)
- You want an automation plan you can execute in phases (quick wins → deeper wins)

For **agency partners** who want repeatable packaging and delivery:

- Standardize what you sell
- Build a reliable "delivery playbook"
- Define what is included vs out of scope

---

## What We Evaluate

During the assessment, FIT looks for repeatable, high-frequency work such as:

- Repetitive admin tasks and copy/paste work
- Manual data entry or double-entry between tools
- Approval bottlenecks and "waiting" steps
- Scheduling and coordination workflows
- Document creation, routing, filing, and retrieval
- Customer inquiries and internal request handling
- Recurring reporting and operational routines
- Tasks staff consistently avoid or dislike (often a high ROI signal)

We also explicitly identify:

- Where **human judgment** must remain in control
- Where AI can assist safely (classification, summarization, retrieval)
- Where automation is **not** appropriate (risk, ambiguity, or unclear rules)

---

## What You Provide (Low-Effort Inputs)

- A list of daily/weekly tasks (rough is fine)
- The tools involved (Microsoft 365, Google, CRM, accounting, etc.)
- A few examples (screenshots, forms, emails, templates — sanitized if needed)
- Known bottlenecks or pain points
- Business priorities (what matters most right now)

You do not need documented processes. FIT extracts the workflow by asking the right questions.

---

## How the Assessment Works

### Step 1 — Workflow review
Structured conversation: what happens, who does it, what triggers it, what "done" looks like, where it breaks.

### Step 2 — Workflow mapping
FIT documents steps and dependencies, tools, owners and handoffs, exceptions and failure points.

### Step 3 — Opportunity identification
Each workflow categorized as: immediate candidate / medium-effort opportunity / longer-term improvement.

### Step 4 — ROI and effort scoring
Scored on: time savings, error reduction, customer impact, complexity, risk, and required guardrails.

### Step 5 — Roadmap delivery
A clear, prioritized plan ready to execute.

---

## Deliverables

### 1) Ranked automation opportunity list
Prioritized with: why it matters, expected outcome, relative complexity and risk, guardrails needed.

### 2) Estimated ROI (time-focused)
Time currently being lost, where the best savings are, which wins deliver fast impact.
> ROI estimates are illustrative and vary by volume, hourly cost, and workflow quality.

### 3) Workflow notes and assumptions
What we observed, what we assumed, what needs confirmation before build.

### 4) Tool and integration considerations
Systems to connect, data quality constraints, integration complexity, optional vs required items.

### 5) Suggested implementation order
Quick wins first → deeper multi-system workflows → nice-to-haves.

### 6) 30/60/90 Day Roadmap
- **30 days:** quick wins + stability
- **60 days:** expansion and standardization
- **90 days:** deeper automation and governance maturity

---

## Typical Findings

Many SMBs uncover:

- 5–20 strong automation opportunities
- 10–40 hours/month lost to manual work
- Duplicate effort across systems
- Several workflows ready for immediate automation
- Clear opportunities for AI-assisted workflows (with guardrails)
- Processes that benefit from standardization before automation

---

## What Happens After the Assessment

- **Option A:** Implement priority automations (highest-value workflows built first)
- **Option B:** Ongoing automation support (manage, monitor, and improve under a recurring model)

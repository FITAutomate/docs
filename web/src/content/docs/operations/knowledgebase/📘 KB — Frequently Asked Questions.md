# Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ covers the most common questions SMB owners and agency partners ask when exploring FIT Automate.

---

## General: What FIT Automate Does

### What exactly is workflow automation?

Workflow automation replaces repetitive manual steps with reliable, repeatable processes. If your team is clicking, copying and pasting, re-typing, chasing updates, or doing the same steps every day or week—it's often automatable.

### What kinds of tasks can be automated?

Most repetitive, rule-based, or data-driven tasks can be automated, including:

- Lead capture and routing
- Scheduling and reminders
- Approvals and internal requests
- Reporting and summaries
- Document generation and filing
- Inbox triage and prioritization
- Syncing data across tools

### What can't be automated?

Anything requiring constant human judgment, sensitive relationship decisions, or unclear rules may need **human-in-the-loop** steps. The goal isn't to automate everything—it's to remove repetitive work while keeping humans in control where it matters.

### Do we need to change our tools or software?

Usually no. FIT Automate works with the tools you already use. The goal is to reduce manual work without forcing a platform change.

### What's the difference between automation and AI?

- **Automation** handles steps and rules.
- **AI** helps with interpretation—language, classification, summarization, retrieval.

Most real workflows use both: automation for structure, AI for flexibility—with guardrails.

## Getting Started

### What is the first step to work with FIT?

A short Discovery Call (or chat). We learn your tools, your pain points, and what a good first win looks like.

### Can we do this over chat, or does it have to be a call?

Either works. Many clients start in chat and only do a call if needed to confirm scope or edge cases.

### What happens after discovery?

The most common next step is an **AI Readiness Audit**—a practical roadmap showing what to automate, where the risk is, and what to do first.

### Do you offer a quick-win option instead of an audit?

Yes, sometimes. If the opportunity is obvious and low-risk, we may recommend a small quick-win pilot first. If the situation is more complex, the audit prevents wasted effort.

### What do you need from us to get started?

Usually just:

- Your top repetitive tasks
- The tools involved
- 1–3 examples (sanitized screenshots, forms, emails, templates)
- Who owns approvals

We keep prep light.

## AI Readiness Audit

### What is an AI Readiness Audit?

A structured review where we map key processes, identify automation and AI opportunities, estimate time savings, and produce a ranked action plan with guardrails and recommended next steps.

### What do we get at the end of the audit?

A practical pack including:

- The best workflows to automate first (ranked)
- Estimated time saved (illustrative)
- Risks, exceptions, and guardrails
- Recommended approach (quick win, project, or ongoing support)
- A phased roadmap (typically 30/60/90 days)

### Do we need documented processes before the audit?

No. Most clients don't have them documented. FIT extracts the workflow by asking the right questions and mapping it simply.

### Is the audit technical?

No. It's business-first: what happens, who does it, what triggers it, what "done" looks like, and where it breaks.

## Time, Delivery, and Disruption

### How long does it take to build automations?

Many workflows can be designed and delivered in **3–10 business days per workflow**, depending on scope and tools. Multi-system or higher-governance workflows can take longer.

### Will automation disrupt our current processes?

FIT introduces changes gradually, tests safely, and avoids interrupting daily operations. Nothing goes live until it's validated.

### Do we need to be hands-on during the build?

No. We keep prep light and do the heavy lifting. You validate outcomes before anything goes live.

### How do you prevent breaking something important?

We design clear triggers, add safeguards, validate with real examples, and use approvals and human checkpoints when risk is higher.

## Security and Access

### Is automation safe for our data?

Yes—when designed properly. FIT follows security best practices and builds workflows to be controlled, observable, and non-destructive by default.

### Do you need our passwords?

No. FIT does not ask for passwords. If access is needed, we use secure methods you control—role-based access, invites and guest access, approved connectors, and credential vaulting where applicable.

### Do you support Microsoft 365 / Outlook?

Yes. FIT often works in Microsoft ecosystems (Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive). We can also work with Google Workspace depending on your environment.

Yes—see **"Trust Center Lite — Security & Privacy Overview"** in the public KB.

## AI Assistants and Knowledge Systems

### Can you build an AI assistant for our business?

Yes. FIT builds scoped AI assistants that answer questions using your approved documents—SOPs, policies, onboarding docs, FAQs, and sales material.

### How accurate are AI answers?

Accuracy is highest when the assistant is scoped to approved content and designed retrieval-first. FIT focuses on "answers backed by your content," not AI guesswork.

### Can an AI assistant be customer-facing?

Yes—with guardrails. For external use, we restrict scope, keep answers consistent, and route edge cases to a human.

### What if we don't have good documentation yet?

That's common. A knowledge assistant can start small (an FAQ plus a few key SOPs) and improve over time.

## Support and Maintenance

### Are automations difficult to maintain?

Most are stable once running. Some workflows need occasional tweaks when tools or processes change.

### What if something breaks?

FIT designs workflows with error handling and alerts. If you're on a support plan, issues are triaged and addressed based on severity.

### Can we take over management ourselves later?

Yes. FIT can document workflows, hand over admin access where applicable, and leave clear operating guidance.

## Pricing and ROI

### How much does automation cost?

It depends on complexity. Simple automations can be quick and approachable; advanced workflows and AI systems take more time. Book a call for a scoped estimate.

### Can you give a ballpark price?

We avoid publishing prices because scope varies. Discovery and the AI Readiness Audit exist to provide clarity without guesswork.

### How fast do we see ROI?

Many teams see ROI in days or weeks because time savings add up quickly. The best workflows often pay for themselves quickly when volume is high.

### Can we start small?

Absolutely. Most clients start with 1–2 workflows and expand when value is proven.

Yes. FIT can support agencies via a **co-branded delivery model ("Powered by FIT")**, with an optional higher-tier **white-label** track for qualified partners.

No. FIT augments delivery capacity and helps you standardize quality. Your agency remains the relationship owner unless you choose a different model.
